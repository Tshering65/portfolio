Project Name: RideSharing App

Problem Statement

Students at our college struggle with high taxi fares due to a lack of coordination in ride-sharing. Many pay 400 Nu alone while taxis leave with empty seats. There is no centralized system to match students traveling at the same time, leading to unnecessary expenses and inefficiency. We need a mobile app that allows students to easily find, join, or create ride requests, enabling cost-sharing and convenience.

Goals

  • Allow students to post ride requests (where & when they are traveling).
  • Enable students to join existing rides instead of booking alone.
  • Provide a simple & secure way to coordinate rides.
  • Give taxi drivers an option to accept shared rides.

User Research

Overview

To understand the needs, pain points, and expectations of both student passengers and taxi drivers, a UX research study was conducted. The study aimed to gather insights before the app’s beta launch, ensuring it addresses real user challenges and offers a smooth experience.

Research Objectives

  • Discover user expectations for ride-sharing features
  • Identify usability issues in booking and navigation flow
  • Improve communication between drivers and passengers
  • Evaluate accessibility and language usability

Research Methods

We conducted mixed-method research:

  • Usability Testing – Users completed key tasks like booking a ride
  • Surveys – Collected feedback on usability and satisfaction
  • Interviews – Explored deeper user needs and frustrations

Participants

A total of 15 participants were involved:

  • 10 students from GCIT
  • 5 taxi drivers from Thimphu

Key Findings

  • ❌ No ride cancellation option disappointed many users
  • 📞 Lack of a call/chat feature between users and drivers led to uncertainty

Key Metrics (KPIs)

  • ✅ 80% booking success rate
  • 🚫 20% drop-off during booking

Recommendations

  • Include a Cancel Ride option
  • Add in-app chat functionality

User Personas

User personas help us build products that meet real user needs and solve actual problems, making our product more successful.

Empathy Map

An empathy map helps you step into your user’s shoes, so you can build products that are not only usable but also meaningful and emotionally resonant.

User Journey Map

A user journey map is a visual or narrative tool that shows the steps a user takes to accomplish a goal with your product or service.

User Research & Key Insights

Student Pain Points

  • Expensive fares – Paying 400 Nu alone instead of splitting.
  • No coordination system – No way to find others traveling at the same time.
  • Manual ride arrangements – Students rely on WhatsApp or word-of-mouth.
  • Unreliable taxi sharing – Some taxis leave half-empty, making costs unfair.

Taxi Driver Insights

  • Prefer full taxis – Maximizing earnings is difficult when students book alone.
  • Need a system to match passengers – Reduces waiting time for full bookings.

Conclusions

The Campus Ride-Sharing App is a user-centered solution designed to tackle a very real and relatable problem faced by college students—high transportation costs due to poor ride coordination. By applying design thinking, particularly through empathy mapping and user journey analysis, the app directly addresses students’ frustrations, anxieties, and hopes. It offers a smart, efficient, and secure way to connect students and taxi drivers, encouraging cost-sharing and reducing environmental impact through fewer redundant trips. With key features like ride creation, real-time matching, notifications, chat support, and driver registration, the app not only simplifies ride arrangements but also fosters a more connected and collaborative student community. Ultimately, this solution has the potential to improve campus life by making commuting more affordable, convenient, and socially engaging.

Project Name: Campus Cake Ordering Website

Problem Statement

A talented home baker—the spouse of a college lecturer—makes custom cakes for students on campus. Despite high demand, the ordering process is currently manual: students have to call or send WhatsApp messages to place orders. This often leads to confusion, delays, or miscommunication. Students feel awkward or uncertain about how to place custom orders or check availability.

Goals

  • Display available cakes and allow easy ordering.
  • Avoid unnecessary phone or message interactions.
  • Provide clarity on pickup timing and pricing.
  • Make ordering comfortable and fast for students.

User Research

Overview

To improve the ordering experience for homemade cakes on campus, a UX research study was conducted. The study included usability testing, surveys, and interviews with students who frequently order cakes.

Research Objectives

  • Understand current challenges in ordering cakes manually via WhatsApp or phone calls.
  • Evaluate usability and clarity of the new website interface.
  • Determine if a structured order form increases user confidence.
  • Identify additional features to improve future versions.

Research Methods

Mixed-method research was employed:

  • Usability Testing – Students placed mock orders using both WhatsApp and the new website.
  • Interviews – Semi-structured interviews explored emotional and functional barriers.
  • Surveys – Captured ease of use and confidence ratings before and after using the site.

Participants

10 college students aged 18–24 with prior cake ordering experience participated.

Key Findings

  • Awkwardness and uncertainty in initiating orders via WhatsApp.
  • Confusion about cake availability and pickup times.
  • Anxiety about order confirmation and correctness.

Key Metrics (KPIs)

  • Reduced order completion time using the website.
  • Increased confidence and satisfaction post-ordering.
  • Lower drop-off rates on the structured order form.

Recommendations

  • Include a structured online order form with cake selection, pickup scheduling, and notes.
  • Provide an order confirmation page or message to reassure users.
  • Ensure the site is mobile-friendly for quick access.

User Personas

Students ordering cakes often feel anxious and uncertain. They prefer clear, easy-to-use systems that avoid awkward phone or message interactions.

Project Name: CampEase Website

Problem Statement

Campers—especially first-timers and students—lack a centralized, reliable platform to buy affordable, quality camping gear. This leads to wasted time, stress, and sometimes poor camping experiences.

Goals

  • Sell curated, essential camping gear in one place.
  • Educate users on what to pack.
  • Offer quick, student-friendly checkout and delivery.
  • Highlight product quality and affordability.

User Research & Key Insights

Interviews with college students and campers revealed several pain points:

  • Difficulty finding reliable gear in one place.
  • Uncertainty about product quality or what’s essential.
  • Last-minute panic buying or missing items due to poor planning.
